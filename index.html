About the Event

Africa's Premier Evaluation Conference

Uganda Evaluation Week is the flagship annual event of the Uganda Evaluation Association (UEA), bringing together evaluation professionals, policy makers, development practitioners, and researchers from across Uganda, Africa, and the world to advance the culture and practice of monitoring, evaluation, accountability, and learning (MEAL).

  • 5 days of keynotes, workshops, and knowledge exchange
  • 200+ evaluation professionals and policy makers
  • Regional case studies and evidence-sharing sessions

    14:00 – 15:30 PM
    Dr. Grace Mulindwa

    Celebrate the very best in Ugandan and East African evaluation practice at the UEA Awards for Evaluation Excellence — recognising outstanding evaluations, evaluators, and organisations that have made a transformative contribution to evidence-based development.

Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Uganda Evaluation Week?
Uganda Evaluation Week (UEW) is the flagship annual conference of the Uganda Evaluation Association (UEA). It brings together evaluation professionals, policy makers, government officials, development practitioners, academics, and students to advance the culture and practice of monitoring, evaluation, accountability, and learning (MEAL) in Uganda and across Africa.
Who should attend Uganda Evaluation Week?
UEW 2026 is open to anyone working in or interested in monitoring and evaluation, development planning, policy, research, or public sector governance. This includes M&E officers, programme managers, government planners, donor representatives, academics, consultants, and students in related fields.
How can I register for UEW 2026?
You can register online through the UEA website or by contacting the UEA Secretariat directly at info@uea.or.ug. Early registration is encouraged as places are limited. Group and institutional packages are also available for organisations wishing to send multiple delegates.
Can I submit a paper or present at UEW 2026?
Yes! UEW welcomes abstract submissions for paper presentations, panel discussions, and poster displays. The call for abstracts is open to practitioners, researchers, and students. Accepted presenters receive a discounted registration rate. Visit the UEA website or email info@uea.or.ug for submission guidelines.
Is accommodation available at the venue?
Silver Springs Hotel offers on-site accommodation at preferential rates for UEW 2026 delegates. We recommend booking early to secure availability. The UEA Secretariat can assist international delegates with accommodation coordination and airport transfers.
Will sessions be available online for those who cannot attend in person?
Yes. UEW 2026 offers a Virtual Delegate option providing access to live-streamed plenary sessions, keynote addresses, and selected workshops. Virtual delegates also receive on-demand recordings after the event. Register as a Virtual Delegate to join from anywhere in the world.
